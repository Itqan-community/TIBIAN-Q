# Scrum Master Intern - ITQAN Community

## About ITQAN Community

ITQAN is on a mission to democratize access to Quranic knowledge through innovative, AI-native technology. We're building the next generation of Quranic learning tools that serve millions of Muslims worldwide using Agile methodologies to deliver value iteratively and continuously.

Our engineering teams work in Scrum sprints, shipping features every two weeks that make the Quran more accessible and understandable. We need passionate facilitators who can help these teams perform at their best.

## What You'll Do

### Run Scrum Ceremonies (40% of time)

**Daily Standups** (Every day, 15 minutes):
- Facilitate quick sync across development team
- Ensure everyone shares: "What I did, what I'm doing, any blockers?"
- Track impediments and follow up on resolution
- Keep meeting focused and time-boxed

**Sprint Planning** (Every 2 weeks, 2-4 hours):
- Help team commit to sprint goals
- Facilitate story estimation and capacity planning
- Ensure product backlog is ready and prioritized
- Clarify acceptance criteria and technical questions

**Sprint Reviews** (Every 2 weeks, 1-2 hours):
- Organize demos of completed features
- Coordinate stakeholder attendance and feedback
- Celebrate team accomplishments
- Document feedback for product backlog

**Sprint Retrospectives** (Every 2 weeks, 1 hour):
- Facilitate team reflection on what went well and what didn't
- Create safe space for honest feedback
- Capture action items for process improvement
- Track follow-through on previous retro commitments

**Backlog Refinement** (Weekly, 1-2 hours):
- Support Product Manager in story clarification
- Help team estimate story complexity
- Identify dependencies and technical questions
- Ensure next sprint's work is ready

---

### Remove Impediments & Support Team (30% of time)

**You're the blocker remover**:
- Team needs access to a tool? You coordinate it.
- Dependency on another team? You facilitate the connection.
- Unclear requirements? You bridge to Product Manager.
- Technical question for architect? You arrange the conversation.
- Conflict between team members? You mediate respectfully.

**Your goal**: Keep the team focused on building, while you handle coordination and obstacles.

---

### Track Metrics & Drive Improvement (20% of time)

**Sprint Metrics**:
- Update Jira/Linear boards with accurate task status
- Monitor burndown charts and velocity trends
- Track sprint completion rates and identify patterns
- Generate sprint reports for stakeholders

**Process Improvement**:
- Implement action items from retrospectives
- Suggest process optimizations based on data
- Research Agile best practices and share with team
- Document team workflows and Scrum practices

---

### Foster Agile Culture (10% of time)

**Servant Leadership**:
- Model Agile values: transparency, collaboration, respect
- Coach team on self-organization and ownership
- Promote continuous improvement mindset
- Celebrate wins and learn from failures
- Build psychologically safe team environment

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Do I need Scrum certification to apply?**
A: For **paid internships**, basic Scrum knowledge required (certification preferred but not required). For **volunteers**, no certification needed—just interest in learning!

**Q: Do I need technical or coding background?**
A: No! Scrum Masters don't write code. Basic understanding of software development is helpful but can be learned on the job.

**Q: What's a typical day like?**
A: You'll facilitate ceremonies (standups, planning, reviews, retros), track metrics in Jira/Linear, remove team blockers, and work on process improvements. Very people-focused, coordination-heavy work.

**Q: Can I get Scrum certified through this internship?**
A: Yes! We provide study resources for CSM/PSM certification. Paid internships may include exam fee sponsorship.

**Q: Will I work with one team or multiple teams?**
A: Typically you'll support one development team (6-10 engineers) to start. As you grow, you might support multiple teams.

**Q: What schedule flexibility do I have?**
A: Moderate flexibility. You'll need to be available for Scrum ceremonies (standups, planning, reviews, retros), but other work is async. Core overlap hours typically 10 AM - 2 PM Egypt time.

**Q: Can I transition from volunteer to paid internship?**
A: Absolutely! Volunteers who demonstrate strong facilitation skills and build Scrum knowledge are prioritized for paid positions.

**Q: What career paths exist after this internship?**
A: **Scrum Master** (most common), **Product Owner**, **Agile Coach**, **Project Manager**, **Engineering Manager**. High performers may be offered full-time roles.

**Q: Do I need to be in Egypt?**
A: Egypt-based preferred for time zone alignment, but global applicants welcome. The role is 100% remote.

**Q: Do I need to be Muslim?**
A: No, but you should respect Islamic values and be genuinely passionate about serving the Muslim community.

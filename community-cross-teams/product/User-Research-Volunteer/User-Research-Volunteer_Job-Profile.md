# User Research Volunteer

## Role Overview
The User Research Volunteer conducts research to understand the needs, behaviors, and pain points of Muslims using ITQAN's Quranic technology products. This role gathers insights through user interviews, surveys, usability testing, and analytics to inform product decisions and ensure we're building products that truly serve the global Muslim community.

## Key Responsibilities

### User Research & Testing (40% of time)
- Conduct user interviews with diverse Muslim users globally
- Facilitate usability testing sessions for new features
- Observe user behavior and identify pain points
- Test prototypes and gather feedback before development
- Conduct diary studies and longitudinal research
- Recruit participants for research studies

### Data Analysis & Insights (25% of time)
- Analyze user feedback and research data
- Identify patterns and themes in user behavior
- Synthesize findings into actionable insights
- Create user personas and journey maps
- Develop empathy maps and problem statements
- Track and analyze product usage metrics

### Research Planning & Design (20% of time)
- Design research studies and methodologies
- Create discussion guides and survey questionnaires
- Plan usability test scenarios and tasks
- Define research objectives and success criteria
- Select appropriate research methods for questions
- Coordinate research scheduling and logistics

### Communication & Collaboration (10% of time)
- Present research findings to product and design teams
- Create research reports and presentations
- Share insights with stakeholders across organization
- Collaborate with Product Designers on user needs
- Work with Product Managers on feature validation
- Document research findings in knowledge base

### Continuous Learning (5% of time)
- Stay updated on UX research best practices
- Learn about Muslim user demographics and needs
- Study Islamic learning behaviors and preferences
- Research accessibility standards for diverse users
- Explore new research tools and techniques

## Required Skills

### Research Methods
- **Qualitative Research**: User interviews, ethnographic studies, contextual inquiry
- **Quantitative Research**: Surveys, A/B testing, analytics analysis
- **Usability Testing**: Moderated and unmoderated testing
- **User Personas**: Creating evidence-based user personas
- **Journey Mapping**: Mapping user experiences and touchpoints

### Research Tools
- **Survey Tools**: Google Forms, Typeform, SurveyMonkey
- **Analytics**: Google Analytics, Mixpanel, Amplitude
- **Testing Tools**: UserTesting, Maze, Lookback
- **Interview Tools**: Zoom, Google Meet for remote interviews
- **Documentation**: Notion, Miro, FigJam for research synthesis

### Analysis & Synthesis
- **Data Analysis**: Identifying patterns in qualitative and quantitative data
- **Insight Generation**: Translating research into actionable insights
- **Storytelling**: Communicating findings compellingly
- **Critical Thinking**: Asking the right questions
- **Problem Framing**: Defining problems from user perspective

### Domain Knowledge
- **Islamic Content**: Understanding of Quranic learning needs
- **Muslim Users**: Knowledge of global Muslim user diversity
- **Arabic Language**: Basic understanding of Arabic (helpful)
- **Cultural Sensitivity**: Respectful research with Muslim participants
- **Accessibility**: Inclusive research practices

### Soft Skills
- Empathy and active listening
- Strong written and verbal communication
- Collaborative and team-oriented
- Detail-oriented and organized
- Curiosity and intellectual humility
- Patience with diverse participants
